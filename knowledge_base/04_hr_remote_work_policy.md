## 1. Purpose & Scope

This Remote Work Policy establishes XYZ Analytics' expectations, standards, and guidelines for employees working outside of a company office, whether full-time, hybrid, or occasionally. It replaces the interim remote work guidance issued in 2022 and reflects the company's permanent stance on distributed work following a comprehensive review led by Marcus Webb (HR Director) and Chloe Brennan (HR Business Partner) in Q4 2024.

**Policy Owner:** Marcus Webb, HR Director
**Approver:** Sarah Mitchell, CEO
**HR Operations Contact:** Yuki Tanaka, HR Coordinator

This policy applies to all full-time and part-time XYZ Analytics employees globally. It does not apply to contractors or vendors, who are governed by their respective agreements. Where local employment law is more restrictive or prescriptive than this policy, local law takes precedence. Questions about jurisdiction-specific requirements should be directed to Marcus Webb.

This policy should be read in conjunction with the **Security & Access Control Policy (SEC-ACCESS-001)**, which defines the technical controls employees must comply with when working remotely, and the **HR Onboarding Guide (HR-ONBOARD-001)**, which describes the equipment provisioning and access setup process for new hires.

## 2. Policy Philosophy

XYZ Analytics believes that great work happens when talented people have the autonomy, tools, and trust to do their best work — wherever that is. We are a remote-first company, meaning that our processes, tooling, and culture are designed to work equally well for distributed and in-person employees. We do not believe proximity to an office is a proxy for performance.

At the same time, we recognize that some roles, projects, and moments benefit from in-person collaboration. Our policy is not about mandating presence or absence — it is about making deliberate, thoughtful choices about when and where we work, and holding ourselves to clear standards regardless of location.

These principles inform every provision of this policy:

- **Outcomes over activity:** We measure what people produce, not when or where they produce it.
- **Deliberate in-person time:** When we gather in person — for team offsites, onboarding, or critical project work — it should be intentional and high-value, not performative.
- **Equity across locations:** Remote employees must have equal access to information, career development opportunities, and visibility with leadership as in-office employees.
- **Shared responsibility for culture:** Every employee — remote or in-person — is responsible for maintaining the inclusive, collaborative culture that makes XYZ Analytics work.

## 3. Eligibility & Approval Process

### 3.1 Who Is Eligible

Remote work eligibility is determined by role type and tenure:

- **Fully Remote Eligible:** All individual contributor roles in Engineering, Data, and Product are eligible for fully remote work arrangements from day one, subject to manager approval.
- **Hybrid Required:** Team leads, Engineering managers, and employees managing direct reports are expected to be in a company office at least 4 days per calendar month, or more frequently during onboarding of new team members.
- **On-Site Required:** Roles explicitly designated as on-site (currently no active XYZ Analytics roles are on-site only) require office presence as defined in the job description.
- **New Hires (first 90 days):** All new employees are expected to work from a company office or co-working location during their first 30 days to support onboarding, relationship-building, and tool familiarity. Exceptions require VP-level approval.

### 3.2 Approval Process

The process for establishing a remote or hybrid work arrangement is as follows:

1. Employee discusses preferred arrangement with their direct manager during the offer stage (new hires) or during a 1:1 (existing employees requesting a change).
2. Manager assesses feasibility based on role requirements, team coverage needs, and timezone considerations.
3. If approved, manager notifies HR via the People Ops intake form (managed by Yuki Tanaka).
4. HR records the arrangement in the HRIS and triggers the relevant onboarding checklist (automated via Slack bot, per **TECH-61**, owned by Chloe Brennan).
5. If the arrangement is denied, the employee may escalate to the HR Business Partner (Chloe Brennan) within 10 business days for a second review. Final escalations go to Marcus Webb.

### 3.3 Annual Review

All remote work arrangements are reviewed annually as part of the performance review cycle. Managers confirm or update arrangements in the HRIS. Significant changes to an employee's role, team, or timezone situation may trigger an earlier review.

## 4. Work Arrangements — Definitions

XYZ Analytics recognizes the following formal work arrangement types:

**Fully Remote**
The employee works entirely from a location of their choice (subject to Section 10 for international arrangements). They are not expected to commute to a company office for day-to-day work. They are expected to attend in-person events as described in Section 5.4.

**Hybrid — Employee-Led**
The employee chooses which days to work remotely and which days to come into the office, with no fixed in-office requirement beyond the minimums set for their role tier. The default for eligible individual contributors.

**Hybrid — Schedule-Fixed**
The employee and manager agree on specific in-office days (e.g., Tuesdays and Thursdays). Used where team collaboration patterns benefit from predictable overlap.

**Temporarily Remote**
An employee whose normal arrangement is in-person or hybrid who is working remotely for a defined period (e.g., while traveling, during a family situation, or due to illness). No formal approval required for periods under 4 weeks; HR notification required for longer durations.

## 5. Remote Work Expectations

### 5.1 Working Hours & Availability

XYZ Analytics does not mandate specific working hours for remote employees, with the following exceptions:

- Employees are expected to be available and responsive during their team's core overlap hours. For most teams, this is **10:00 AM – 3:00 PM in the timezone of the employee's team lead**.
- Calendar must accurately reflect availability, including focus time blocks, out-of-office periods, and deep work windows. Inaccurate calendars that cause meeting conflicts or missed handoffs are considered a collaboration failure.
- Response time expectations by channel are as follows:
  - Slack (direct message): within 2 hours during core hours
  - Slack (channel message): within 4 hours during core hours
  - Email: within one business day
  - Urgent/flagged Slack messages from manager or skip-level: within 30 minutes during core hours

### 5.2 Workspace Requirements

Employees working remotely are responsible for maintaining a workspace that supports focused, professional work. Requirements include:

- A stable internet connection with a minimum of 25 Mbps download / 10 Mbps upload. A backup connection option (mobile hotspot) is strongly recommended.
- A quiet environment suitable for video calls. Background noise that disrupts team meetings is the employee's responsibility to mitigate (noise-cancelling headphones, virtual backgrounds, or a dedicated workspace).
- Adequate lighting for video calls during business hours.
- A private space for calls that involve confidential information — particularly relevant for HR, Legal, and senior leadership roles.

### 5.3 Childcare & Dependent Care

XYZ Analytics understands that many employees manage caregiving responsibilities alongside work. We do not prohibit employees from working from home while caring for dependents, provided that:

- Care responsibilities do not routinely prevent the employee from meeting availability and responsiveness expectations during core hours.
- The employee's work quality and output are not adversely impacted over a sustained period.

Employees who anticipate a period of increased care demands (e.g., new child, family illness) are encouraged to speak proactively with their manager and HR (Derek Fountaine or Chloe Brennan) about flexible arrangements.

### 5.4 In-Person Requirements

Even fully remote employees are expected to travel to company or team events as follows:

- **All-Hands (twice per year):** All employees are expected to attend. Location alternates between the company's primary offices. Travel and accommodation are company-funded.
- **Team Offsites (once per year per team):** Team leads (e.g., Aisha Patel for DevOps, Laura Hensley for Data, Daniel Cruz for Engineering) are responsible for planning annual team offsites. Attendance is expected unless exceptional circumstances apply.
- **Onboarding Support:** Managers, buddy mentors, and key collaborators for new hires are expected to be in-person with new team members for at least the first week of that employee's tenure, regardless of their normal work arrangement.

Travel expenses for required in-person events are reimbursed per the XYZ Analytics Travel & Expenses Policy.

## 6. Home Office Standards & Equipment

### 6.1 Company-Provided Equipment

All XYZ Analytics employees receive the following standard equipment, provisioned by IT (coordinated by Yuki Tanaka):

- MacBook Pro (14" or 16", depending on role tier)
- External monitor (27", standard)
- Mechanical keyboard and mouse
- Noise-cancelling headset (Jabra or equivalent)
- Webcam (if not using a standalone monitor)

Equipment requests outside the standard package require manager approval and are submitted via the IT request portal.

### 6.2 Home Office Stipend

Employees on fully remote or hybrid-employee-led arrangements receive:

- **Initial setup stipend:** $750 one-time, available after 90 days of employment, reimbursable for desk, chair, monitor stand, lighting, or other ergonomic equipment.
- **Annual recurring stipend:** $250 per year for consumables, accessories, and workspace upgrades.

Stipends are processed via the expense reimbursement system. Receipts are required for all claims. Stipends are non-transferable and do not roll over year-to-year.

### 6.3 Internet Reimbursement

Fully remote employees are eligible for reimbursement of up to **$60 per month** toward their home internet service, submitted monthly via the expense system.

## 7. Security & Data Handling Obligations

Remote employees are subject to the full requirements of the **Security & Access Control Policy (SEC-ACCESS-001)** at all times. The following obligations are highlighted here for emphasis, given their heightened relevance in remote work contexts:

- **VPN mandatory:** All access to XYZ Analytics internal systems, data infrastructure, or production environments must be via the company VPN. No exceptions.
- **No public Wi-Fi for sensitive work:** Employees must not access Tier 3 or Tier 4 data (as defined in **DATA-GOV-001**) from public Wi-Fi networks (cafés, airports, hotels) even with VPN active. A mobile hotspot is the minimum acceptable alternative.
- **Screen locking:** Computers must auto-lock after 5 minutes of inactivity. Manual locking is required whenever stepping away from the workspace in a shared environment.
- **No unauthorized data transfer:** Company data must not be copied to personal devices, personal cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox personal accounts), or personal email accounts.
- **Secure disposal:** Hardware nearing end-of-life must be returned to IT via the equipment return process coordinated by Yuki Tanaka. Employees must not personally dispose of or repurpose company equipment.

Any suspected security incidents — including lost devices, unauthorized access, or phishing attempts — must be reported immediately to the Security team (Aisha Patel) and HR (Marcus Webb) per the incident response procedures in the **Incident Management SOP (OPS-INC-001)**.

## 8. Communication & Collaboration Standards

### 8.1 Default Communication Norms

XYZ Analytics operates on the following default communication principles for distributed teams:

- **Default to async:** Written updates, Loom recordings, and documented decisions are preferred over synchronous calls for non-urgent work. This respects focus time and enables global timezone participation.
- **Document decisions:** Any significant decision made in a meeting, Slack thread, or 1:1 must be summarized and stored in the relevant Notion page, Jira ticket, or shared doc within 24 hours.
- **Over-communicate status:** Remote employees should err on the side of sharing progress updates, blockers, and context changes — particularly when work is paused or redirected.

### 8.2 Meeting Norms

- All recurring team meetings must have a written agenda shared at least 24 hours in advance.
- Video cameras are encouraged but not mandatory during meetings. Cameras are expected during 1:1s and performance conversations.
- Meetings should have a note-taker assigned in advance. Notes must be distributed to all attendees (and relevant absent stakeholders) within 24 hours of the meeting.
- No meeting should be scheduled during the first or last 30 minutes of the core overlap window (protecting heads-down start and end of day).

### 8.3 Headcount Planning Dashboard

The HR team, in collaboration with Product, is developing a headcount planning dashboard in InsightDash (TECH-62, owned by Derek Fountaine) to provide real-time visibility into open roles, attrition metrics, and hiring pipeline status. This will improve distributed decision-making by giving managers self-serve access to workforce data without requiring manual reports from HR.

## 9. Performance Management for Remote Employees

Performance management at XYZ Analytics is identical for remote and in-office employees. Outcomes, collaboration quality, and demonstration of company values are the basis for performance assessment — not location or hours logged.

### 9.1 Goal Setting

All employees set quarterly OKRs in alignment with their team's goals. OKRs are documented in the company-wide OKR tool and reviewed in monthly 1:1s between employee and manager.

### 9.2 Performance Review Cycle

Formal performance reviews are conducted twice per year (June and December). The cycle includes:
1. Employee self-assessment (submitted to HR via HRIS 2 weeks before review window).
2. Manager assessment, incorporating peer feedback collected via the 360 feedback process.
3. Calibration session attended by all people managers, facilitated by Marcus Webb.
4. Review conversation between manager and employee.
5. Compensation review (where applicable), approved by department VP and Sarah Mitchell.

### 9.3 Underperformance

Remote employees on a performance improvement plan (PIP) will be asked to increase in-person time with their manager during the PIP period to facilitate closer support and more frequent feedback. This is a support mechanism, not a punitive measure.

## 10. International & Cross-Border Remote Work

### 10.1 Working Abroad (Short Term)

Employees may work from another country for up to **30 consecutive days** per calendar year without requiring HR pre-approval, provided:
- The destination country does not create a permanent establishment risk for XYZ Analytics.
- The employee maintains compliance with data handling obligations in Section 7.
- The employee's manager is informed of the dates and location.

Stays exceeding 30 consecutive days, or any work from a country with known PE risk, require written approval from Marcus Webb and review by the company's employment law counsel.

### 10.2 Permanent International Relocation

Employees wishing to permanently relocate to a new country must submit a formal request to HR at least 60 days before the planned move date. XYZ Analytics will assess:

- Feasibility of employment in the destination country (entity presence, employment law, tax obligations).
- Compensation adjustment requirements (if any) based on local market benchmarks.
- Equipment, security, and data compliance implications.

XYZ Analytics currently employs staff in the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, and Germany. Relocations to these countries are generally supportable. Relocations to other countries are assessed case-by-case.

## 11. Health, Wellbeing & Inclusion

XYZ Analytics is committed to ensuring remote employees have equitable access to wellbeing resources and feel genuinely included in the company's culture.

Remote-specific wellbeing provisions include:

- **Mental health support:** All employees have access to the EAP (Employee Assistance Program), which provides up to 8 free counselling sessions per year. Details are shared during onboarding (see HR-ONBOARD-001) and are available from Chloe Brennan at any time.
- **Home ergonomics:** Employees who experience discomfort from their home setup can request an ergonomic assessment, funded by the company. Contact Yuki Tanaka to arrange.
- **Social connection:** Managers are responsible for creating regular informal touchpoints for their teams — virtual coffee chats, team social calls, or equivalent — at least twice per month.
- **Isolation awareness:** HR conducts a quarterly pulse survey to identify employees who may be experiencing social isolation or disengagement. Survey results are reviewed by Marcus Webb and shared in aggregate with people managers.

## 12. Policy Violations

Violations of this policy — including security non-compliance, sustained unavailability during core hours, or unauthorized international work — will be handled through the standard HR disciplinary process:

1. Informal conversation between manager and employee (for first-time or minor violations).
2. Formal written warning issued by HR (Marcus Webb or Chloe Brennan) for repeated or serious violations.
3. Performance Improvement Plan for sustained non-compliance.
4. Termination in cases of willful, serious, or repeated security violations.
